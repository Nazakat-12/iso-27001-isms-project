## \## Incident Response Procedure

## 

## \### 1. Purpose

## 

## To define the steps required to prepare for, detect, analyze, contain, eradicate, and recover from information security incidents to minimize impact on business operations.

## 

## \### 2. Incident Handling Phases

## 

## | Phase | Key Activities | Responsible Team |

## | :--- | :--- | :--- |

## | \*\*P1: Preparation\*\* | Ensure tools, contacts, and runbooks are current. | IT Operations / Security Team |

## | \*\*P2: Detection \& Triage\*\*| Monitor SIEM alerts; Initial assessment of severity and impact. | Tier 1 Analysts |

## | \*\*P3: Containment\*\* | Isolate affected systems; Disable compromised accounts; Apply temporary patches. | Security \& Network Teams |

## | \*\*P4: Eradication\*\* | Identify the root cause; Remove malware/vulnerabilities. | Security \& Development Teams |

## | \*\*P5: Recovery\*\* | Restore systems from trusted backups; Monitor systems for recurrence. | IT Operations |

## | \*\*P6: Lessons Learned\*\* | Conduct a post-incident review (PIR); Update procedures and controls. | ISMS Steering Committee |

## 

## \### 3. Communication

## 

## For \*\*High Severity\*\* incidents, the Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) must be notified within \*\*1 hour\*\* of detection. Customer notification is required within 48 hours, depending on regulatory requirements (e.g., GDPR).
